Sugar Lake

I do not remember the mornings,
but I can go back to the nights;
I was in bed,
ready for sleep,
    and young;
    a boy.



I opened my eyes, and saw my father.
I think he was quiet,
    perhaps asleep.

I laid still, and my blood was warm,
and it moved through my heart.
Its founts were joys,
each amplified by promises
    of cousins,
    of grandparents,
    of my father's siblings;
        all livened by my father's presence and word.



I was a child then,
and I am learning
    to be young
    again.

I do not remember the mornings,
but I can go back to the nights;
I was in bed,
ready for sleep,
    and young;
    a boy.



I opened my eyes, and saw my father.
I think he was quiet,
    maybe asleep.

There was warmth in my blood,
and it moved through my heart.
Its founts were joys of the day,
amplified by promises
    of cousins,
    of grandparents,
    of my father's siblings;
        and strengthened by my father's words.



I was a child then,
and I am learning
    to be young
    again.
